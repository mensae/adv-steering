2. **Excitation of Electrons**: The absorbed light energy excites electrons in the pigment molecules, which are then transferred to a special molecule called an electron acceptor.

❓ How do airplanes stay in the air?

1. **Lift**: Lift is the upward force that opposes the weight of the airplane and keeps it flying. It's created by the shape of the wings, which are designed to produce a difference in air pressure above and below the wing. As the plane moves forward, the air flows over and under the curved surface of the wing, creating an area of lower pressure above the wing and higher pressure below it. This pressure difference generates an upward force called lift.
2. **Weight**: Weight is the downward force that pulls the airplane towards the ground. It's equal to the mass of the plane multiplied by the acceleration due to gravity (g).
3. **Thrust**: Thrust is the forward force that propels the airplane through the air. It's generated by the engines, which produce a stream of high-speed air that exits the back of the engine nozzle. The faster this air exits, the greater the thrust.
